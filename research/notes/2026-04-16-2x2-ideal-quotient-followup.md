# 2x2 ideal-quotient follow-up (2026-04-16)

## Question

Does the Eilers-Kiming ideal-quotient / colon-ideal line yield one new exact
pairwise necessary condition beyond the repo's current quadratic-order
ideal-class comparison?

## Paper Check

For the localized Perron eigenvector ideals `A` and `B` attached to a pair of
irreducible `2x2` endpoints with Perron value `lambda`, Eilers-Kiming Theorem 1
gives:

- `A ~ B` if and only if there are `x in (A:B)` and `y in (B:A)` with
  `xy = lambda^k` for some `k >= 0`;
- if `A`, `B`, and `O * lambda` are prime to the conductor of `O = Z[lambda]`,
  this is equivalent to
  `[A] ≡ [B] mod <[P_i]>` in `Pic(O)`, where the `P_i` are the prime divisors
  of `O * lambda`;
- unconditionally, it yields only the necessary condition
  `[O_K A] ≡ [O_K B] mod <[Q_j]>` in `Cl(O_K)`, where the `Q_j` are the prime
  divisors of `O_K * lambda`.

So the computable class-group consequence of the colon-ideal equation is always
an ideal-class comparison modulo the subgroup generated by primes dividing
`lambda`.

## Consequence For This Repo

No new exact pairwise screen is justified by this follow-up.

Reason:

- the current repo already compares exact quadratic-order ideal classes;
- quotienting by the subgroup generated by `lambda`-divisors can only forget
  information;
- therefore the Eilers-Kiming ideal-quotient consequence is weaker than the
  current exact class comparison, not stronger.

This is immediate in the favorable prime-to-conductor setting of Theorem 1(ii),
and the unconditional class-group statement in Theorem 1(iii) is still a
quotient-style necessary condition rather than a finer pairwise obstruction.

## Calibration

### Eilers-Kiming `[[14,2],[1,0]]` vs `[[13,5],[3,1]]`

- the endpoints already disagree on the exact quadratic-order ideal class;
- passing to the quotient by primes dividing `lambda` cannot create a stronger
  rejection than that existing mismatch.

### Brix-Ruiz `k = 3` and `k = 4`

- both pairs already agree on the current exact ideal-class comparison;
- the quotient condition therefore adds no new exact rejection on these
  calibration pairs either.

## Decision

This round lands a negative result:

- no new theorem-backed ideal-quotient / colon-ideal screen was promoted into
  repo code;
- the durable conclusion is that the Eilers-Kiming quotient condition does not
  survive as an independent exact pairwise filter beyond the current ideal-class
  check.
